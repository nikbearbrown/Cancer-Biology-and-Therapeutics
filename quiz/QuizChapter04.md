# Chapter 4 Quiz: Genetics and Genomic Instability in Cancer

## Instructions
Choose the best answer for each question. Each question tests understanding of key concepts from genetics and genomic instability in cancer.

---

**Question 1:** What is the accuracy of DNA replication in terms of error frequency?

a) One incorrect base per thousand nucleotides

b) One incorrect base per million nucleotides

c) One incorrect base per billion nucleotides

d) One incorrect base per trillion nucleotides

---

**Question 2:** The small DNA fragments synthesized on the lagging strand during DNA replication are called:

a) Leading fragments

b) Okazaki fragments

c) Helicase fragments

d) Primase fragments

---

**Question 3:** A proto-oncogene is best described as:

a) A gene that is permanently activated and causes cancer

b) A normal gene that helps cells grow and divide, but can become an oncogene when mutated or overexpressed

c) A gene that always suppresses cell division

d) A gene that only functions during embryonic development

---

**Question 4:** According to Knudson's "two-hit hypothesis" for tumor suppressor genes, what is required for tumor development?

a) Activation of one allele of a tumor suppressor gene

b) Inactivation or mutation of both alleles of a tumor suppressor gene

c) Activation of both alleles of an oncogene

d) Mutation of only one allele in any gene

---

**Question 5:** The TP53 gene is known as the "guardian of the genome" and is mutated in approximately what percentage of all cancers?

a) About 10%

b) About 25%

c) About 50%

d) About 75%

---

**Question 6:** What is aneuploidy in the context of cancer?

a) The presence of too many nucleotides in DNA

b) An abnormal number of chromosomes in cancer cells

c) The loss of all chromosomes

d) Normal chromosome number with structural changes only

---

**Question 7:** The Philadelphia chromosome, a hallmark of chronic myeloid leukemia (CML), results from:

a) Deletion of chromosome 9

b) Duplication of chromosome 22

c) Translocation between chromosomes 9 and 22

d) Inversion of chromosome 21

---

**Question 8:** Which DNA repair pathway is primarily responsible for fixing pyrimidine dimers caused by UV radiation?

a) Base excision repair

b) Mismatch repair

c) Nucleotide excision repair

d) Homologous recombination

---

**Question 9:** Xeroderma pigmentosum is a hereditary cancer syndrome caused by defects in which DNA repair mechanism?

a) Base excision repair

b) Mismatch repair

c) Nucleotide excision repair

d) Double-strand break repair

---

**Question 10:** Approximately what percentage of cancers have lost or gained at least one chromosome (aneuploidy)?

a) About 25%

b) About 50%

c) About 75%

d) About 90%

---

## Answer Key

1. **c)** One incorrect base per billion nucleotides

2. **b)** Okazaki fragments

3. **b)** A normal gene that helps cells grow and divide, but can become an oncogene when mutated or overexpressed

4. **b)** Inactivation or mutation of both alleles of a tumor suppressor gene

5. **c)** About 50%

6. **b)** An abnormal number of chromosomes in cancer cells

7. **c)** Translocation between chromosomes 9 and 22

8. **c)** Nucleotide excision repair

9. **c)** Nucleotide excision repair

10. **d)** About 90%
