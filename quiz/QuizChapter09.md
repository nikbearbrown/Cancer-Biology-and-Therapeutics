# Chapter 9 Quiz: Cell Cycle Control and Cancer

## Instructions
Choose the best answer for each question. Each question tests understanding of key concepts from cell cycle control and cancer.

---

**Question 1:** What are the four discrete phases of the eukaryotic cell cycle?

a) G0, G1, G2, and M

b) M, G1, S, and G2

c) Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, and Telophase

d) Interphase, Mitosis, Cytokinesis, and G0

**Question 2:** Cyclin-dependent kinases (CDKs) require binding to which regulatory protein to become active?

a) p53

b) Rb protein

c) Cyclins

d) p21

**Question 3:** Which CDK inhibitor families regulate mammalian cell cycle progression?

a) Only the Cip/Kip family

b) Only the Ink4 family

c) Both Cip/Kip and Ink4 families

d) The p53 and Rb families

**Question 4:** The "restriction point" in G1 phase is primarily controlled by complexes of which proteins?

a) CDK1 and cyclin B

b) CDK2 and cyclin E

c) CDK4/6 and cyclin D

d) CDK7 and cyclin H

**Question 5:** What is the primary function of the p53 tumor suppressor protein in response to DNA damage?

a) It directly repairs damaged DNA

b) It promotes cell cycle arrest, DNA repair, and apoptosis

**Question 6:** Approximately what percentage of human cancers contain a mutation in the p53 gene?

a) About 10%

b) About 25%

c) About 50%

d) About 75%

**Question 7:** The Rb (retinoblastoma) protein functions as a "brake" on cell cycle progression by:

a) Activating cyclin-CDK complexes

b) Binding and inhibiting E2F transcription factors when hypophosphorylated

c) Promoting DNA replication

d) Destroying p53 protein

**Question 8:** What is the primary mechanism by which DNA tumor viruses (such as HPV, SV40, and adenoviruses) promote cancer development?

a) They directly mutate DNA through insertional mutagenesis

b) They sequester and inactivate tumor suppressor proteins like Rb and p53

c) They activate oncogenes through chromosomal translocations

d) They cause inflammation leading to DNA damage

**Question 9:** Palbociclib (Ibrance) was the first FDA-approved drug in which class of targeted cancer therapeutics?

a) Tyrosine kinase inhibitors

b) CDK4/6 inhibitors

c) PARP inhibitors

d) Checkpoint kinase inhibitors

---

**Question 10:** The G2/M checkpoint prevents entry into mitosis in the presence of unreplicated or damaged DNA primarily through which kinase?

a) CDK1

b) Chk1

c) ATM

d) Wee1

---

## Answer Key

1. **b)** M, G1, S, and G2

2. **c)** Cyclins

3. **c)** Both Cip/Kip and Ink4 families

4. **c)** CDK4/6 and cyclin D

5. **b)** It promotes cell cycle arrest, DNA repair, and apoptosis

6. **c)** About 50%

7. **b)** Binding and inhibiting E2F transcription factors when hypophosphorylated

8. **b)** They sequester and inactivate tumor suppressor proteins like Rb and p53

9. **b)** CDK4/6 inhibitors

10. **b)** Chk1
