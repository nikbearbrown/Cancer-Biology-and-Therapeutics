# Chapter 8 Quiz: Cancer Etiology

## Instructions
Choose the best answer for each question. Each question tests understanding of key concepts from cancer etiology.

---

**Question 1:** What percentage of cancers are directly attributable to environmental and lifestyle factors?

a) About 30%

b) About 50%

c) About 70%

d) More than 90%

---

**Question 2:** The multistep process of chemical carcinogenesis consists of three distinct stages established by Berenblum in 1982. What are these stages?

a) Mutation, selection, and metastasis

b) Initiation, promotion, and progression

c) DNA damage, repair failure, and transformation

d) Inflammation, proliferation, and invasion

---

**Question 3:** How many well-established carcinogens are present in each puff of cigarette smoke?

a) More than 10

b) More than 30

c) More than 60

d) More than 100

---

**Question 4:** Ionizing radiation has been called a "universal carcinogen" because:

a) It only affects cancer cells

b) It will induce cancer in most tissues of most species at all ages, including the fetus

c) It is the most common cause of cancer worldwide

d) It only causes one specific type of cancer

---

**Question 5:** Approximately what percentage of all human cancers have been attributed to viral infections?

a) About 2%

b) About 12%

c) About 25%

d) About 40%

---

**Question 6:** What percentage of cervical cancer cases are caused by persistent infection with oncogenic strains of HPV?

a) About 50%

b) About 75%

c) About 85%

d) Virtually all cases (95-100%)

---

**Question 7:** Helicobacter pylori infection is associated with which type of cancer?

a) Liver cancer

b) Gastric (stomach) cancer

c) Colorectal cancer

d) Esophageal cancer

---

**Question 8:** What is the global prevalence of Helicobacter pylori infection in adults?

a) Close to 10%

b) Close to 25%

c) Close to 50%

d) Close to 75%

---

**Question 9:** The two HPV oncoproteins that play key roles in cervical carcinogenesis by targeting tumor suppressor proteins are:

a) E1 and E2

b) E4 and E5

c) E6 and E7

d) L1 and L2

---

**Question 10:** Which statement best describes the current understanding of cancer prevention?

a) Most cancers are genetic and cannot be prevented

b) Cancer prevention is only effective through early detection

c) Vaccination against oncogenic viruses and elimination of chemical exposures represent effective prevention strategies

d) Environmental factors play no role in cancer prevention

---

## Answer Key

1. **d)** More than 90%

2. **b)** Initiation, promotion, and progression

3. **c)** More than 60

4. **b)** It will induce cancer in most tissues of most species at all ages, including the fetus

5. **b)** About 12%

6. **d)** Virtually all cases (95-100%)

7. **b)** Gastric (stomach) cancer

8. **c)** Close to 50%

9. **c)** E6 and E7

10. **c)** Vaccination against oncogenic viruses and elimination of chemical exposures represent effective prevention strategies
